Genetics - from then to now→

  1. 1859 Charles Darwin wrote the "The Origin of Species" where he proposes his natural selection theory.
  2. 1865 Gregor Mendel’s experiments on peas shows that heredity is transmitted in discrete units.
  3. 1869 Frederick Miescher isolates DNA and calls it “nuclein”.
  4. 1879 Walter Flemming stains chromosomes to observe them during cell division and describes the whole process of mitosis.
  5. 1902 Walter Sutton observes the segregation of chromosomes in meiosis.
  6. 1909 Wilhelm Johannsen uses the word “gene” to describe Mendel's unit of heredity.
  7. 1911 Thomas Hunt Morgan and his student study fruit flies and discover that chromosomes carry genes.
  8. 1943 William Astbury suggests that DNA have a regular periodic structure with nucleotide bases stacked on top of each other.
  9. 1944 Barbara McClintock, working with corn, discovers that genes can move around on chromosomes and that the genome can be dynamic.
  10. 1953 Francis H. Crick and James D. Watson report the double helix structure of DNA.
  11. 1955 Joe Hin Tjio finds that the human cell has the exact number of 46 chromosomes.
  12. 1958 Matthew Meselson and Franklin Stahl discover that DNA replicates semiconservatively.
  13. 1959 Jerome Lejeune and his colleagues discover that Down Syndrome is caused by trisomy 21, three copies of chromosome 21,
  14. 1961 Sydney Brenner, François Jacob and Matthew Meselson discover that mRNA acts as a messenger between the DNA in the nucleus and the protein-making machinery in the cytoplasm.
  15. 1966 Marshall Nirenberg and others crack the genetic code of nucleic acids and that their 4 letter alphabet determine the order of 20 kinds of amino acids in proteins.
  16. 1973 Researchers clone an animal gene from the African clawed frog Xenopus.
  17. 1975 DNA sequecencing methods are developed where colored dyes identify the four nucleic acids of DNA.
  18. 1981 Scientists create the first transgenic mice and fruit flies.
  19. 1982 Genentech, the first genetic engineering company, markets the first artificial drug, human insulin.
  20. 1983 Researchers discover the genetic marker for Huntington's disease on chromosome 4.
  21. 1983 The polymerase chain reaction is invented. It allows scientists to make billion copies of a specific segment of DNA.
  22. 1989 Repetitive DNA sequences known as microsatellites are used as genetic landmarks to distinguish between people.
  23. 1990 The Human Genome Project is launched. The goal is to identify all of the approximate 30,000 genes in human DNA.
  24. 1994 The FDA approves the sale of FLAVR SAVR tomato, the first genetically modified food.
  25. 1995 Ban on genetic discrimination in the workplace.
  26. 1997 Ian Wilmut and colleagues from the Roslin Institute in Scotland cloned Dolly the sheep.
  27. 2004 A vaccine for Human Papillomavirus is created to protect women against infection with HPV 16 and 18. It inactivates tumor suppressor genes and together cause 70% of cervical cancers.
  28. 2007 Michael Worobey analyzed the the genetic mutations of HIV and found that infections had started in the USA as early as the 1960s.
  29. 2013 Scientists use the CRISPR–Cas9 system to slice the genome in human cells at the precise sites of their choosing.
  30. 2016 NASA astronaut Kate Rubbins sequenced a genome in outer space for the first time aboard the International Space Station.
